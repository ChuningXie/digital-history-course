# Data Licenses and Citations

The datasets provided in this folder (`1_Open_Access_Data`) are curated exclusively for educational and academic research purposes within the Digital History course. The instructor does not hold the copyright to these materials; all rights remain with the original institutions, projects, and creators.

When utilizing these datasets for analysis in tools such as Gephi, Voyant, ArcGIS, or OpenRefine, and when incorporating them into your final group projects, you must adhere to the following licensing agreements and provide proper formal citations.

## 1. Open Access Data

### The Old Bailey Online
* **Source:** The Proceedings of the Old Bailey, 1674-1913.
* **License:** Provided under a **Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 International (CC BY-NC 4.0)** license. Use is strictly limited to non-commercial, educational, and research purposes. 
  *(Note: The project explicitly permits computational use such as Topic Modelling and Named Entity Recognition, but strictly prohibits the use of its transcribed text for training, fine-tuning, or evaluating Large Language Models).*

* **Required Citation Formats:**
  When citing Old Bailey data in your assignments, please use the exact formats below, ensuring you include the current site version (version 9.0, Autumn 2023):

  **1. To cite the project as a whole (General dataset reference):**
  > Tim Hitchcock, Robert Shoemaker, Clive Emsley, Sharon Howard and Jamie McLaughlin, et al., *The Old Bailey Proceedings Online, 1674-1913* (www.oldbaileyonline.org, version 9.0, Autumn 2023).

  **2. To cite a specific Trial (If you extract specific texts for close reading):**
  > *Old Bailey Proceedings Online* (www.oldbaileyonline.org, version 9.0, Autumn 2023), [Month Year], trial of [Defendant's Name] ([Trial Reference Number]).
  > *(Example: Old Bailey Proceedings Online, April 1754, trial of Elizabeth Canning (t17540424-60).)*

  **3. To cite a specific Search or Statistical query:**
  > *Old Bailey Proceedings Online* (www.oldbaileyonline.org, version 9.0, Autumn 2023), searched for [quote the exact search phrase/parameters as they appear in the results].

### Freedom on the Move (FOTM)
* **Source:** Freedom on the Move: A Database of Fugitives from North American Slavery (Cornell University et al.)
* **License:** Provided under a **Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 International (CC BY-NC 4.0)** license. This is a crowdsourced academic project. Data is provided strictly for educational and scholarly research; commercial use is explicitly prohibited.

* **Required Citation Formats:**
  When citing FOTM data in your assignments, please use the following formats:

  **1. To cite the project/database as a whole:**
  > *Freedom on the Move: A Database of Fugitives from North American Slavery*, Cornell University, accessed [Date of access], https://database.freedomonthemove.org/.
  > *(Example: Freedom on the Move: A Database of Fugitives from North American Slavery, Cornell University, accessed March 7, 2026, https://database.freedomonthemove.org/.)*

  **2. To cite a specific runaway advertisement:**
  > Runaway advertisement for [Name of the Freedom Seeker], *[Name of the Original Newspaper]*, [Publication Date]. *Freedom on the Move* Database, accessed [Date of access], [Insert specific advertisement URL if applicable].
  > *(Example: Runaway advertisement for Moses, North-carolinian, January 25, 1856. Freedom on the Move Database, accessed March 1, 2026).*

## 2. Restricted Data (IPUMS & ProQuest)

Due to strict Terms of Service, user agreements, and copyright protections, this GitHub repository **does not contain** raw or processed data from **IPUMS** or **ProQuest**. Redistribution of these datasets is strictly prohibited by their respective platforms.

To work with these materials, you must download the raw datasets individually using your own registered accounts or through the Binghamton University Libraries system. You can then use the Python scripts provided in the `2_Restricted_Data_Scripts` folder to process the data locally on your own machine. 

---

## 3. Data Ethics and Academic Integrity

Working with historical data requires rigorous attention to data provenance and ethics. Passing off pre-processed data from restricted databases as your own original data collection, or failing to properly cite open-access datasets, is a serious violation of academic integrity. 

Please be aware that Harpur College has requested that any students found violating the honesty policy will have to sign an admission of academic dishonesty. This document will go directly on your internal student record here at BU. Always ensure your data sources are transparent, correctly attributed, and legally accessed.
